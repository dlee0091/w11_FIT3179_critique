Insights into Global Unemployment Data

This dashboard provides visual insights into global unemployment rates, visualised through various charts. It highlights trends and disparities across regions, age groups, and genders to offer a comprehensive view of the unemployment challenge.

Global Map Visualisation

This map displays unemployment rates globally for the selected year. Darker shades represent higher unemployment rates, allowing for quick visual comparisons between countries.

Bubble Plot

The bubble plot visualises various regions with corresponding unemployment figures. The size of each bubble represents the unemployment rate, making it easy to identify regions with significant unemployment challenges.

This Bubble Plot provides a visualisation of unemployment rates across different regions. Each bubble represents a region, and the size of the bubble correlates with the region's unemployment rate. Larger bubbles indicate higher unemployment rates, making it easier to identify regions that face more significant unemployment challenges.

Sabah stands out with the largest unemployment rate at 7.9%, making it the region with the most severe unemployment issues. On the other hand, despite having the largest population, Selangor maintains a relatively low unemployment rate of 2%, which is comparable to Perlis, also at 2%. Interestingly, Kedah has one of the lowest unemployment rates, standing at 1.9%, highlighting that some regions with fewer people may still achieve lower unemployment rates.

Radial Chart

This radial chart showcases the top 10 countries with the highest unemployment rates, segmented by sex. The colour-coded segments represent different countries, illustrating the disparities in unemployment rates between males and females.

The Radial Chart provides a clear visualisation of the top 10 countries with the highest unemployment rates. Each segment corresponds to a specific country, with its size representing the unemployment rate for the selected sex. The chart allows users to easily compare unemployment rates between men and women by switching the selection using the dropdown below the chart.

A key insight from the chart is that both males and females hold the highest unemployment rates in Djibouti, underscoring significant gender-neutral economic challenges in the country. The colour-coded segments, explained in the legend, help to quickly identify countries with similar rates.

Grouped Bar Chart

This chart compares unemployment rates by gender and age group in Malaysia. Each colour represents a different age group, allowing for an analysis of how unemployment affects various demographics.

The Grouped Bar Chart shows unemployment rates in Malaysia across both gender and age groups. The comparison between female and male unemployment across different age ranges highlights how unemployment impacts these demographics.

Over the years, female unemployment has been consistently higher than male unemployment, particularly among younger age groups (15-24).
