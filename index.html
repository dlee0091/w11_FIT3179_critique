Unemployment Around the World

From the shadows of high unemployment rates in Sub-Saharan Africa to pockets of economic stability in Northern Europe, this map gives a global perspective. Did you know? Over 20 countries are struggling with unemployment rates above 10%. In contrast, some regions in Asia and Oceania are enjoying rates as low as 2%.

Regional Giants and Challenges

Explore the key regions where unemployment is disproportionately high. In Malaysia, W.P. Labuan and Sabah stand out with higher rates, while states like Putrajaya are faring much better. Compare the regions easily through this visual lens, with each bubble representing the weight of unemployment in different parts.

Unemployment and Gender: A Global Divide

Across many countries, gender-based unemployment gaps reveal stark contrasts. For example, in several countries like Botswana and Eswatini, women experience significantly higher rates of unemployment compared to men. This comparison brings gender disparities into sharp focus, particularly in regions hit hardest.

Unemployment Rates by Age and Gender

A closer look at Malaysia shows stark differences in unemployment rates across gender and age groups. Youth aged 15-24 face unemployment rates as high as 16%, while the figures are significantly lower for older age groups. Discover how different demographic segments compare in the job market.

Malaysia's Employment Landscape

In Malaysia, Sabah leads with the highest unemployment rate at 7.9% in early 2024. This map highlights which states are thriving and which are struggling.
